Movie Booking Website Design
Case Study

Booking Store is an Online Movie Ticket Booking System is a website to provides the customer's facility to book tickets for a movie online and to gather information about the movies and theaters.

  • Project Duration : November 2021 - December 2021
  • The Problem : During a movie, customers are not able to reserve seats or book any potential extra services.
  • The Goal : Design a website to allow customers to easily reserve a seat and make any necessary bookings at the Booking store.
  • My Role : Solo UX/UI Designer
  • Responsibilities : Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs

User Research: Summary

I conducted user interviews and unmoderated research on five people. Most of the research was conducted online. Based on my assumptions, I assumed that all movie theaters would show the same movie and that all of the users would have a credit/debit card to book a seat and purchase a ticket. It was possible to pay with a method other than a credit card/debit card. I had to adjust some payment and working methods based on feedback to make it more accessible and acceptable to all kinds of users.

User Research: Pain Points

1. Time - In the cinema, customers are too busy to spend time purchasing movie tickets.
2. Seat Reservation - A traditional movie ticket does not require a seat reservation, which limits the movie experience at the cinema.
3. Accessibility - Booking platforms do not offer comprehensive accessibility options.
3. Food & Drinks - Customers need to arrive early at the cinema to pick the food and drink they want to bring.

Persona: Alex
Problem Statement

Alex is a movie watcher who needs to buy a ticket for a movie on a particular date and time because he can’t find a movie on a particular date and time.

Usability Study: Findings

These were the main findings uncovered by the usability study :


1. Booking - Users want to book a ticket quickly.
2. Food & Drinks - Users want to pick the food and drink in the booking.
3. Payment - Users want more payment methods

Accessibility Considerations

1. Provided access to users who are vision impaired through adding alt text to images for screen readers.
2. Used icons to help make navigation easier.
3. Used detailed imagery for food, drinks and devices to help all users better understand the designs.
